# Canonical Website Narrative — State of Paul v2

## One-line positioning

Paul Gradie is a research-trained software engineer, platform leader, and product builder who turns ambiguous, complex systems into reliable production software.

## Expanded positioning

Paul should be presented as a rare hybrid:

- A **scientist** trained to reason from uncertainty, evidence, experiments, and long-term system behavior.
- A **staff-level software engineer** with production depth across .NET, platform systems, data science, ML/NLP, cloud, developer tooling, and distributed teams.
- A **platform and product leader** who can turn ambiguous domains into plans, architectures, delivery rhythms, and durable outcomes.
- An **AI-fluent builder** who understands applied AI, AI-enabled engineering, agent workflows, and production constraints without making “AI model builder” the headline claim.
- A **founder/operator** building practical products and experiments through Launch11 and related projects.
- A **communicator/teacher** who writes about engineering, AI, team dynamics, science, career systems, and product thinking.

## Strategic correction from v1

Do not position Paul primarily as someone who helps companies build AI models.

Paul has serious AI credibility, but the current website should lead with software/platform/product leadership. AI should appear as a capability layer, not as the whole offer.

The site should say, in effect:

> Paul brings scientific reasoning, production engineering discipline, applied-AI fluency, and product judgment to complex software problems.

It should not say, directly or indirectly:

> Paul’s main commercial offer is building foundational AI models for companies.

## Core themes to weave through the website

### 1. Scientist → Engineer → Platform/Product Leader

The story is not “I changed careers.” It is “I carried a scientist’s way of thinking into software.”

Use this framing:

- Biology trained Paul to think about complex adaptive systems.
- Data science and ML/NLP taught him how models interact with real users and production constraints.
- Devtools and platform engineering taught him how systems fail, scale, degrade, and recover.
- Current AI practice brings those threads together inside engineering workflows, product systems, and organizational execution.

### 2. Production reality over theoretical cleverness

Paul’s differentiator is not only intelligence or creativity. It is operational realism:

- resilient systems
- observable systems
- testable systems
- deployment and migration discipline
- incremental delivery
- architecture that survives real teams
- human/social realities of engineering work

### 3. AI as a capability layer, not the identity

AI should be present, but precise:

- AI is a reasoning and execution accelerator.
- AI changes how engineers plan, review, coordinate, and document work.
- Strong fundamentals matter more, not less.
- Codebases need to become legible to both humans and AI.
- AI-enabled engineering requires governance, review, observability, and deliberate workflows.
- Applied AI systems belong inside products and workflows, not just demos.

Do not imply that Paul’s main offer is training foundation models or running a model lab.

### 4. Founder/operator energy

Projects should not read as random side quests. They should read as a consistent product-building laboratory:

- Launch11 as the studio/vehicle.
- Renaym, Writr, Sayr, Siftr, Party Bots, Prompt Library, Qwerky Studio as productized experiments.
- Sailfish, Palavyr, SeqPyPlot as historical proof-of-work.
- Biologically aligned learning experiments as careful research frontier, not a finished commercial claim.

### 5. Teacher and writer

Articles should be positioned as part of Paul’s professional surface:

- AI-enabled engineering
- engineering process
- software design
- human collaboration
- science and explanation
- career and compensation systems

## Things to avoid

Do not over-index on “founder” in a way that weakens staff/principal engineering credibility.

Do not make the site sound like a generic AI consultant landing page.

Do not stuff every memory/fact into the page. Exclude or minimize:

- personal finances
- family details
- compensation specifics, except where already intentionally discussed in the Glass Wall article
- private work specifics
- internal company confidentials
- anything that sounds like exaggerated claims without proof

Do not make every page long. The design works because it is concise. Upgrade the density of signal, not the number of paragraphs.

## Preferred tone

- Clear, confident, direct.
- Slightly personal, but not confessional.
- Senior but not corporate.
- Curious and rigorous.
- Builder with taste and evidence, not guru.
- Excited about AI without sounding breathless or opportunistic.
