# Positioning Decision Record — Website Update v2

## Decision

Update `paulgradie.com` around this corrected positioning:

> Paul Gradie is a research-trained software engineer, platform leader, and product builder who turns ambiguous, complex systems into reliable production software — with applied AI, scientific reasoning, and pragmatic engineering leadership as part of the toolkit.

## Why this decision exists

The previous landing-page direction risked over-centering AI model-building. Paul has genuine AI credibility: a data science / AI engineering background, production ML/NLP experience, an AI/ML patent, AI-native engineering practice, and current AI product/workflow experiments.

However, the most accurate current pitch is not:

> I help companies build AI models.

The more accurate pitch is:

> I help teams turn complex ideas and ambiguous systems into reliable software, practical AI workflows, and useful products.

## Strategic hierarchy

Use this hierarchy across the site:

1. Staff-level software/platform/product engineer.
2. Research-trained systems thinker.
3. Applied-AI / AI-native engineering practitioner.
4. Founder/operator and product builder.
5. Writer, educator, musician, and curious human.

AI should be visible, credible, and current, but it should not swallow the whole identity.

## Claims to avoid or soften

Avoid headline claims such as:

- “I help companies build AI models.”
- “I build foundational AI models.”
- “Frontier AI model builder.”
- “Model lab” as the primary frame.
- “Foundation model development” as a current commercial offer.
- Any copy that suggests Paul’s day-to-day work is primarily pretraining/fine-tuning large AI models.

Acceptable wording:

- Applied AI systems.
- Practical AI workflows.
- AI-enabled engineering.
- AI-native product and engineering workflows.
- Production AI/ML background.
- Model-aware product/platform thinking.
- Scientific reasoning and experimentation.
- Research-to-production engineering.

## Preferred homepage promise

Recommended:

> I build software where research, product, and production meet.

Alternative:

> I help teams turn complex ideas into reliable software.

Supporting copy:

> I’m Paul Gradie — a Melbourne-based software engineer, platform leader, founder, and research-trained builder. I work across product engineering, platform architecture, applied AI, and technical leadership, helping teams move from ambiguous problems to production systems that are testable, observable, maintainable, and useful.

## Practical implication for the coding agent

When updating the site, do not simply add more AI language. Replace imprecise AI language with sharper senior-engineering language.

The website should feel like it belongs to someone who can:

- lead high-trust platform/product engineering work;
- reason from uncertain evidence;
- ship production systems;
- use AI thoughtfully inside real workflows;
- write and teach clearly;
- build products from scratch;
- operate across research, architecture, and execution.
