# Page Brief — Home (`/`, `src/pages/index.jsx`)

## Current page role

The Home page currently has:
- Main nav.
- Eyebrow: Melbourne-based, building for global teams.
- H1: “I turn complex ideas into high-impact software.”
- Short bio describing Paul as software engineer, founder, inventor with PhD background.
- CTAs to Projects, Articles, Download CV.
- Latest Writing.
- Education and Career Snapshot.

## Strategic upgrade

Make the Home page Paul’s executive landing page.

The homepage should **not** imply that Paul’s main offer is helping companies build AI models. It should immediately communicate:

- staff-level software/platform/product engineering;
- scientific/research-trained reasoning;
- production discipline;
- applied-AI fluency;
- founder/product-building energy;
- clear writing and teaching.

## Preferred hero copy

H1:
> I build software where research, product, and production meet.

Subhead:
> I’m Paul Gradie — a scientist-trained staff software engineer, platform lead, founder, and AI-fluent product builder. I help teams turn ambiguous technical problems into resilient software, practical AI workflows, and products that survive contact with reality.

## Alternate hero copy

H1:
> Research depth. Production discipline. Product instinct.

Subhead:
> I work across platform architecture, product engineering, applied AI, and technical leadership, helping teams move from ambiguity to production systems that are testable, observable, maintainable, and useful.

## Recommended sections

1. Hero with CTAs.
2. Four “What I do” cards:
   - Platform architecture and resilient systems.
   - Product engineering from ambiguity to production.
   - AI-enabled engineering and practical workflows.
   - Founder-led product experiments.
3. Latest Writing.
4. Career Snapshot.
5. Optional “Start here” section.

## Avoid

- “I help companies build AI models.”
- “Building foundational AI models.”
- “Applied AI and model-driven products” as a prominent card title.
- Any copy that makes Paul sound like a generic AI consultancy.

## Design guidance

Keep the current minimalism. Use compact cards, not dense paragraphs.
