Global Take-Home Comparison

This component compares the annual take-home experience of the same local nominal salary across Australia, selected U.S. states, Ontario, India, the Philippines, Hong Kong, and New Zealand. It also applies shared assumptions for bonus income, deductions, work-from-home claims, and rental-property interest / capital-works style deductions so you can inspect how the curves bend as income rises.

Method notes

This component uses simplified annual models intended for visual comparison, not filing. The U.S. side uses federal single-filer rules plus state tax and employee payroll taxes. Hong Kong uses salaries tax at progressive rates or standard rate, whichever is lower. India uses the newer slab structure with a salary standard deduction and rebate treatment. The Philippines uses the TRAIN-era graduated income tax table effective from 2023 onward. New Zealand uses the post-1 April 2025 progressive resident rates. Canada uses a simplified federal + Ontario model.

Because the x-axis is local salary, this is a nominal comparison of tax drag, not a purchasing-power or exchange-rate comparison.
