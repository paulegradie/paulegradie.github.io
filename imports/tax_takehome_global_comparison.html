Global take-home comparison lab

This page focuses on the cross-region salary equivalence question: if two people doing the same work earn local headline salaries in different economies, how much actually lands in their bank accounts after modeled tax, and how does that compare under nominal, FX, and purchasing-power lenses. For the question “is A$200k in Melbourne locally like US$200k in Arizona?”, the purchasing-power lens is the closest read on this page.

Nominal: same local headline number, not equivalence
FX: same market-value salary across regions
PPP: closest local-equivalence lens

Method notes

FX mode uses approximate market exchange rates and PPP mode uses simplified purchasing-power or regional-price factors. This page is intentionally wage-focused and does not model rental properties or country-specific property tax strategies. Taxes remain explanatory approximations for a single filer or resident and do not cover every credit, treaty, payroll cap edge case, or local household-cost nuance.
