## Overview

This paper, published in *Phytologia*, describes a new species of mulberry — *Morus murrayana* — from eastern North America. The work is a contribution to the systematics of the genus *Morus* (family Moraceae), which includes the commercially important white mulberry (*M. alba*) and red mulberry (*M. rubra*), as well as several cryptic or recently described taxa in North America.

## Taxonomic Context

The genus *Morus* in North America has a complex taxonomic history. Hybridisation between native and introduced species (particularly the introduced *M. alba* from East Asia) has produced populations with intermediate morphologies that complicate species delimitation. *M. murrayana* is distinguished from congeners by a specific combination of leaf shape, margin characters, fruit morphology, and regional distribution.

## Formal Description

The paper provides:

- Full Latin diagnosis and English description of *Morus murrayana*
- Comparative morphology distinguishing the species from *M. alba*, *M. rubra*, and related taxa
- Type specimen designation and repository information
- Notes on habitat, distribution, and phenology
- A key to eastern North American *Morus* taxa incorporating the new species
